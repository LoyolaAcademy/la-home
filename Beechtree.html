"Indian Beech Tree"

The Indian Beech, or Karanj, is a fast-growing, nitrogen-fixing tree native to the Indian subcontinent.
Known for its oil-rich seeds, this tree is widely cultivated for environmental restoration projects and industrial use.
It is also recognized for its resilience in drought-prone areas.
The tree has cultural importance in rural India, where it is used for medicinal purposes and as a source of fuel.

TREE DESCRIPTION:

Indian Beech trees typically grow up to 15â€“25 meters in height. The tree has a spreading crown, compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and clusters of flowers that are typically pink, purple, or white.
The tree produces large, flat, brown seed pods containing oil-rich seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and tolerates dry, salty soils, making it an excellent choice for soil restoration projects.

USES:

- Bio-diesel Production: Seeds are processed for biodiesel, a renewable source of energy.
- Traditional Medicine: Used for treating skin conditions, ulcers, and rheumatism.
- Soil Restoration: Acts as a nitrogen-fixing tree, enriching the soil.
- Environmental Conservation: Commonly used in reforestation and afforestation projects.
- Insect Repellent: The oil is used in pest control in rural areas.
- Cultural Importance: The tree has symbolic significance in rural areas and is often planted near homes and temples
