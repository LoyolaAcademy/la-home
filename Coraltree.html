"Indian Coral Tree"

The Indian Coral Tree (Erythrina variegata), or Parijat, is a striking tree known for its vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring. Native to tropical Asia and the Pacific Islands, this tree is highly regarded for its ornamental value and cultural significance. The tree is often found in Indian temples and gardens, where it is considered sacred and associated with positive energy. Its vivid flowers attract pollinators and are symbolic of prosperity and joy.

TREE DESCRIPTION:

Indian Coral Tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with thorny branches and trifoliate leaves.
The tree produces large clusters of bright red tubular flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting bees and hummingbirds.
The bark is corky and gray, and the tree bears pods containing hard seeds. The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

USES:

- *Ornamental:* Commonly planted for its striking red flowers and aesthetic appeal.
- *Traditional Medicine:* The bark is used for treating joint pain and inflammation.
- *Cultural Significance:* Used in religious rituals and temple gardens.
- *Soil Conservation:* Helps in controlling soil erosion in tropical regions.
- *Beekeeping:* The tree attracts pollinators, making it valuable for beekeepers.
- *Wood:* The wood is used in some traditional crafts.
