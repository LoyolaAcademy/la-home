"Bael Tree"

Bael (Aegle marmelos), also known as Wood Apple, is a sacred tree in India that holds great religious and medicinal value. The tree is revered for its association with Lord Shiva and is often planted in temples. Its fruit, leaves, and other parts are used in Ayurvedic and traditional medicine to treat various ailments. Bael is also considered a symbol of purity and strength in Indian culture.

The leaves are trifoliate, aromatic, and glossy. The tree produces a round fruit with a hard woody shell and sweet, fibrous pulp inside.
Bael prefers dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sacred groves and temple gardens across India.

USES:

- *Digestive Health:* The fruit is used to treat constipation, diarrhea, and indigestion.
- *Ayurvedic Medicine:* The bark, roots, and flowers are used for fever, jaundice, and respiratory problems.
- *Religious Significance:* Leaves are offered to Lord Shiva during rituals.
- *Diabetes Management:* Bael is beneficial in controlling blood sugar levels.
- *Anti-inflammatory:* The fruit and leaves are used to reduce inflammation and treat skin issues.
- *Antioxidant:* The fruit pulp has antioxidant properties that promote overall health.
